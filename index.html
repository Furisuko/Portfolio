Customer Pain Point
Time-draining lines for bulk buyers frustrate loyal shoppers, ruining the Costco Run!

Solution: Introduce a $400/year Express Lane (EL) membership.

Rationale: Offer all members the option to upgrade for faster, expedited checkout. Target Customers, Goals, Estimates
Target Customers: Time-sensitive customers earning over $125K/year.

Goals: Achieve 3% adoption at pilot store and ensure <5min wait times.

Express Lane Wait Times: 3–5 minutes until reaching the cashier, even at peak hours! Activation Sequence
Idle EL staff assist normal lanes until an EL member arrives.

Member taps EL card → validation triggers staff dispatch.

After serving EL customers, staff return to standard lanes. Phased Rollout Plan
Survey customers in line to validate demand.

Launch EL lottery for fairness and awareness.

Limit 300 EL memberships/month initially; adjust with demand. Financial Forecasts
Pilot annual cost: $57K.

Break-even: 142 members upgrading.

3% adoption = $1,012,500 net profit/store.

Risks: fairness perception, peak surges, survey accuracy.
Challenge
Many tutors conduct very few sessions and work with limited students, driving up costs for recruitment, onboarding, and management.

Impact: High costs restrict ability to reach more disadvantaged students needing free math tutoring. Primary Stakeholder
Tutors delivering free academic support.

Goal: Increase tutor engagement to make the program sustainable while maintaining high-quality, accessible education. Company Goal
Boost session counts and student reach per tutor.

Outcome: Lower costs and enable the organization to support more students with existing resources. Solution
Create an integrated tutor profile page that highlights monthly tutor performance.

Incentive: Students can post non-identifying thank-you messages for tutors meeting activity targets.

Behavior Shift: Recognition motivates tutors to increase sessions and students, reducing overhead and expanding student impact.
